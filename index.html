My Journey

I am a doctoral researcher working in the field of high-energy nuclear and particle physics. My academic path has been driven by curiosity about the fundamental structure of matter and the early universe.

Research Focus

  • Heavy-ion collisions and QGP formation
  • Quarkonium production (J/ψ, ψ(2S), Υ)
  • Small systems: pp, pA, OO collisions
  • Initial-state modeling using Glauber approaches

What I Have Done

I have performed large-scale Monte Carlo simulations, analyzed differential cross sections, multiplicity dependence, and transverse momentum distributions using ROOT. My work contributes to understanding medium effects in heavy-ion and small-system collisions.

Research Interests Ahead

I aim to contribute to experimental data analysis within large collaborations, focusing on quarkonium suppression, collectivity in small systems, and statistical properties of the QCD medium.
